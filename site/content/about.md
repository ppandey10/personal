I am Prasoon Pandey, an incoming PhD student in [Dr. Melike Dönertaş’ group](https://www.leibniz-fli.de/research/research-groups/doenertas) at the [Fritz Lipmann Institute](https://www.leibniz-fli.de/), where I work on the systems biology of aging.

I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Physics at the [Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra](https://www.bitmesra.ac.in/). During this time, I was introduced to theoretical astrophysics and developed a deep interest in statistics, data science, and mathematical modeling. I worked with [Dr. Uma Papnoi](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=JeLsl_4AAAAJ&hl=en) and [Dr. Farruh Atamurotov](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=7-XPshgAAAAJ&hl=ru), leading to my first peer-reviewed [publication](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.dark.2024.101612) on the thermodynamics and gravitational lensing properties of charged black holes in perfect fluid dark matter.

I then pursued a Master’s degree in Computational Physics at [Friedrich-Schiller-Universität Jena](https://www.uni-jena.de/). My thesis focused on building a neural network–based surrogate model to accelerate waveform generation for binary neutron star mergers. This work combined physics-based modeling with machine learning to address computational bottlenecks in gravitational-wave data analysis.

Alongside my thesis, I actively explored interdisciplinary applications of machine learning. As a student researcher with [Dr. Nuno Carvalhais](https://www.bgc-jena.mpg.de/person/121289/2206) at the [Max Planck Institute for Biogeochemistry](https://www.bgc-jena.mpg.de/), I developed spatio-temporal deep learning models to forecast extreme droughts and heatwaves across Africa, working at the interface of climate science and AI.

In early 2025, I joined [Dr. Melike Dönertaş’ group](https://www.leibniz-fli.de/research/research-groups/doenertas) as a HiWi student, where I began investigating the stochastic nature of aging. My work evolved toward developing dynamical systems models of DNA methylation to understand how regulatory networks are perturbed over time and contribute to biological aging. I now continue this line of research as a PhD student, aiming to bridge mechanistic modeling and data-driven approaches in aging research.
